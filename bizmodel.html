Business Model

Unit economics & 12-month projection — Warsaw prototype

💶 €24 avg per visit 👥 250 max capacity 📈 3%/week growth 📅 1→6 days/week ramp 🧑‍💼 4 core staff
Assumptions

Model Inputs

Stress-test scenario for the Warsaw prototype. Opens just 1 day/week, adding one more day each month until reaching 6 days/week (7th reserved for private events). Assumes heavy promotional discounting (40% off list price) and slow organic growth (3%/week). 64 founding members get their first experience free.

💰 Revenue

  • €24 avg per visit (40% promo discount from €40 list)
  • 250 daily capacity (max)
  • 3% weekly growth from 15 visits/day
  • 1→6 days/week ramp (+1 day/month)
  • Month 12 revenue: €40,560 (65 visits × 26 days)

📊 Operating Schedule

  • Month 1: 1 day/week (4 days) — 16 visits/day
  • Month 2: 2 days/week (9 days) — 18 visits/day
  • Month 3: 3 days/week (13 days) — 21 visits/day
  • Month 4: 4 days/week (17 days) — 23 visits/day
  • Month 5: 5 days/week (22 days) — 27 visits/day
  • Month 6+: 6 days/week (26 days) — 30→65 visits/day

7th day reserved for private events (revenue not modeled).

🧑‍💼 Team (4 FTE)

  • Developer / Digital Product Manager
  • Event Manager
  • Physical Construction Builder
  • Operations Manager
  • Avg salary: €45k/yr = €3,750/mo each
  • Total: €15,000/month

🏢 Fixed Costs

  • Rent: €10,000/month
  • Tech equipment: €100,000 (amortized 12mo = €8,333/mo)
  • Buildout: €200,000 (months 6–12 = €28,571/mo)

Year 1 loss: €363k. The operating days ramp (1→6 days/week) means early months generate almost no revenue while fixed costs run full-time. Revenue never catches up to the cost base in Year 1. The path to profitability requires raising prices, accelerating growth, or cutting costs.

Unit Economics

Per-Visit Breakdown

At €24/visit with only 65 visitors on 26 operating days (Month 12), fixed costs dominate. The 1→6 day ramp makes early months especially painful.

  • Buildout amort.: €16.91

🚨 Negative Margin Through Year 1

-€12.63 per visit at Month 12 — losing money on every visitor

Operating only 6 days/week at €24/visit, the business needs ~100 visits/day to cover monthly costs (with buildout), or ~54 visits/day without. At 3%/week growth, that's not reached until ~Month 16–20.

🎯 Future Upside: At Full Price & Scale

If discounts are phased out and growth continues to 250/day (6 days/week = 26 operating days):

  • At 250/day × €40 × 26 days: €260k revenue vs €62k costs → €198k/mo net (76%)
  • At 250/day × €24 × 26 days: €156k revenue vs €62k costs → €94k/mo net (60%)
  • Full capacity at full price reached ~Month 22
Sensitivity

Path to Profitability

🔮 What It Takes to Break Even

  • At €24/visit, 6 days/wk: Need ~100 visits/day. At 3%/week growth → Month 20
  • At €32/visit, 6 days/wk: Need ~75 visits/day → Month 15
  • At €40/visit, 6 days/wk: Need ~60 visits/day → Month 12
  • Key levers: Raising price from €24→€40 saves 8 months. Adding a 7th public day saves ~15% on time-to-breakeven
Discount phase-out strategy

If the 40% promo discount is gradually phased out (€24 → €32 by M6, €40 by M12), the blended Year 1 revenue roughly doubles, turning the €293k loss into a small profit. The promotional period buys market awareness; the question is whether demand sustains as prices rise.

Cost reduction levers

Delay buildout: Removing the €200k Phase 2 buildout saves €28.6k/mo in M6–12. Year 1 loss drops from €363k to €163k.

Reduce team to 3 FTE: Saves €3,750/mo = €45k/yr. Year 1 loss drops to €318k.

Both: Year 1 loss drops to €118k — survivable with modest seed capital and private event revenue from the 7th day.

Founding cohort economics

64 founding members receive their first visit free. At €24/visit, this represents €1,536 in foregone revenue. If each founding member returns 2x/month at €24, that cohort generates €3,072/month ongoing — meaningful when total monthly revenue is €11–47k.

Capital

Funding Requirements

💼 Capital Needed (Stress Case)

  • Tech equipment upfront: €100,000
  • Year 1 operating losses: €363,000
  • Working capital buffer: ~€37,000
  • Total funding needed: ~€500,000

This stress scenario requires ~€500k to survive Year 1. The 1→6 day ramp delays revenue even further. The business doesn't generate positive monthly cash flow until visits reach ~54/day (without buildout) or ~100/day (with buildout) — around Months 16–20 at 3% weekly growth.

This is a fundraising scenario, not a bootstrapping one. The combination of ramping operating days, discounted pricing, and slow growth requires ~€500k and 20 months of patience. The 7th day (private events) represents unmodeled upside that could partially offset losses.

Risks

Key Risks (Stress Scenario)

⚠️ Cash Runway

With €363k in Year 1 losses, the business burns ~€30k/month on average. Without sufficient runway (~€500k), it dies before reaching the growth inflection point.

⚠️ Discount Trap

Promotional pricing at €24 may anchor customer expectations. Raising prices later risks churn. Must plan the transition from promotional to full price carefully.

⚠️ Buildout Timing

Starting €200k buildout at Month 6 — when the business is losing €43k/mo — is aggressive. Consider delaying to Month 12+ when demand is proven and daily losses are narrowing.

⚠️ Growth Stall

If 3%/week growth stalls below ~100 visits/day, the business never reaches monthly breakeven on 6 days/week. The "valley of death" from Month 6–20 is the critical risk zone.
